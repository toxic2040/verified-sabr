# The measured price of deployed CGR practice: route selection under CCSDS 734.3-B-1

J. Councilman — companion note to `docs/notes/sabr-regime-characterization.md` (draft v0.1, 2026-06-05). Distills that draft for DTN implementers and CCSDS working-group members. Every number is taken from a named source: the sections through “The measured price” from the source draft and this repository's committed harness and corpora; the HDTN section from a 2026-07-10 three-way differential run whose findings, minimal reproductions, and exact counts are filed publicly as nasa/HDTN#70 and nasa/HDTN#71 (full run artifacts held by the author, available on request). This note introduces no claim, count, or mechanism absent from those artifacts.

## Deployed practice and the four-key order on paper

Schedule-Aware Bundle Routing (SABR, CCSDS 734.3-B-1) selects among candidate routes to a destination by a fixed lexicographic order (§3.2.8.1.4 a): earliest projected arrival, then fewest contacts, then latest termination time, then smallest entry node number. On paper the order is total and precise.

Deployed Contact Graph Routing practice does not enumerate an unrestricted route class. Both the machine-checked reference in this repository and ION 4.1.4 prune with a visited-contact list during search. Separately, the standard’s text describes the candidate-routes list as complete in one clause (§3.2.5.1 b) and, in another, permits cessation of additional-route computation as an implementation matter (§3.2.6.9.1). ION 4.1.4 is a cessation-reading implementation: in the unloaded regime its route-list machinery stops at the first candidate (every captured computation in 1000 corpus_v3 plans contains exactly one route), so its four-key cascade ranges over a singleton and keys 2–4 are vacuous on that list. The reference populates a multi-route frontier under the completeness reading but still applies visited-list closing. The measured price of those choices is what follows.

## The measured price

Four constructions were graded against one oracle truth on corpus_v3 (cislunar-quantized: 1000 generated LunaNet-style plans, 13 nodes, ~398 contacts each, 4093 found dispatches). Grades are conformant / key-2 excess / key-3 / key-4 of 4093:

| construction                        | conf. | k2  | k3   | k4  |
|-------------------------------------|-------|-----|------|-----|
| reference, recording binary (2-key) | 2020  | 0   | 2024 | 49  |
| reference, 4-key, string entry      | 3777  | 0   | 210  | 106 |
| reference, 4-key, numeric entry     | 3882  | 0   | 210  | 1   |
| ION 4.1.4                           | 2526  | 512 | 1054 | 1   |

Mechanism decompositions in the source account for each residual. The recording binary’s 2024 key-3 deviations split 1841 comparator-caused against 183 closing-caused; the four-key comparator eliminates every comparator-caused case and leaves 210 closing-caused ones, with zero new. On the current reference with numeric entry order, key-3 remains 210 (all closing-caused) and key-4 collapses to 1 (closing-caused). Those 210 key-3 residuals and that single key-4 residual of 4093 dispatches are the visited list’s measured price under the completeness reading.

ION’s 512/1054/1 profile is the swing between the completeness and cessation clauses. The 512 key-2 excesses are the first-Dijkstra construction’s stale hop counts at arrival ties; key 3 is never consulted and ION’s relative key-3 strength is emergent; key 4 is never consulted and contact-index order yields a single deviation. Under the cessation reading the profile is zero by construction: the four-key cascade never ranges over more than one candidate.

On dsn_real_v1 (55 day-plans from two months of archived DSN Now tracking; 8912 dispatches, 6935 found, 1977 none, both verdicts oracle-confirmed two-sided) the complementary profiles converge. The current reference is conformant on 6935/6935. ION’s registered profile, graded in ION’s declared margin frame with the oracles margined identically, is 6905 conformant, key-2 excess 0, key-3 30, key-4 0 — 99.6% conformant in its own frame. A live differential pass confirmed this profile against ION 4.1.4 on every dispatch (8912 dispatches, 6935/6935 route-exact, zero mismatches; `docs/notes/2026-06-07-live-ion-55-plan-validation.md`). On the helio bands the reference is conformant on all 5016.

The remaining cross-frame freedom on the real corpus is the OWLT margin. §3.2.6.5–6 add “the applicable OWLT margin” to every arrival computation and never fix its value. On dsn_real_v1 the margin value alone moves the delivered arrival on 851 of 6935 dispatches (12.3%) relative to a margin-zero deployment. Conformance at key 1 is undefined on those dispatches until an authority outside the standard picks a number.

## A second deployed stack: HDTN v2.0.0

NASA’s HDTN (v2.0.0, `eaf7b38`) carries two routing algorithms in `common/cgr`: classic `dijkstra()`, the production default of `hdtn-one-process`, and `cmr_dijkstra()` (opt-in). A three-way differential against the Lean reference and recorded ION 4.1.4 on corpus_v3 (1000 plans, 4100 queries, byte-identical integer plan data on all legs) measured, first, total agreement where the standard is unambiguous: route/no-route verdict 4100/4100 and exact arrival 4093/4093 (7 agreed no-route) for both HDTN algorithms against both references. Every observed divergence is below key 1.

At key 2 the measured profile reads exactly like ION’s, at higher amplitude: on 2992 of 4093 routed dispatches (73%) classic `dijkstra()` returned a route with a strictly shorter equal-arrival witness (delta versus the witness: mean +2.51 hops, median +2, max +9 — lower bounds on true excess, the witness being frontier-proved only); `cmr_dijkstra()` on 527 (+1.02 mean), measured with the #69 seed fix applied. A 4-contact minimized plan reproduces it with the library arguing against itself — post-#69 `cmr_dijkstra` returns the 2-hop route, `dijkstra()` the 3-hop, same arrival. The code fact beneath the profile: no hop-count comparison exists anywhere in either selection path (relaxation on arrival alone, `libcgr.cpp:415-440`). Whether that is nonconformance turns on the same clause pair as ION’s 512: under §3.2.6.9.1’s cessation license the cascade never ranges over the unmaterialized shorter candidate, and §3.2.8.1.4 is vacuously satisfied; under the completeness reading of §3.2.5.1 b) it is a key-2 gap. HDTN thus re-measures the candidate-list degree of freedom on a second codebase — and adds the datum that the four-key comparator itself was never written. The 25 dispatches where recorded ION was the longer one at equal arrival (dijkstra and the reference agreeing on the shorter route) are ION’s already-characterized equal-arrival residue seen from a second angle.

The corpus sits ≥30 s from window boundaries by construction, so a probe set was built where it cannot see: contact end exactly at earliest transmission. There the two HDTN algorithms disagree with each other — on a 3-contact plan, `cmr_dijkstra` returns a route whose relay transmits at the closing instant (arrival 203) while `dijkstra()` returns no route for the identical query; with a later window available, `cmr_dijkstra` switches sides and skips to it. Three code points carry the split: strict end in `dijkstra()` (`libcgr.cpp:415`), an inclusive viability gate in `cmr_dijkstra` (`:654`), and a `contact_search_index` fallback that returns the last window with no feasibility re-check (`:559-576`). The reference reads §3.2.4.1.1’s exclusion sentence literally (“no contact whose end time is *before* its earliest transmission time”), which admits the closing instant. Unlike the deferrals cataloged below, this sentence is not silent at the boundary — it speaks, and it admits equality. Classic `dijkstra()`’s strict end is therefore an implementation departure (or an undeclared engineering margin), and `cmr_dijkstra`’s half-inclusive behavior an implementation inconsistency, not textual freedom. One codebase, two semantics, one clear sentence: the only finding in this note’s ledger that lands on the implementation side of its own dividing line.

Both findings are filed upstream — nasa/HDTN#70 (hop-count key, with a 4-contact minimized reproduction) and nasa/HDTN#71 (boundary split, 3-contact reproduction, verified on stock v2.0.0 and with the #69 fix applied); nasa/HDTN#69, from the same differential instrument, precedes them. The issues carry the counts and repro plans verbatim.

## Why this is a property of the text

Measured disagreement between independently developed, faithful implementations concentrates on five places the text leaves open. In the source’s audit frame, no measured disagreement traces to anything other than these deferred points or a since-fixed implementation defect with a named mechanism.

**Candidate-list population.** §3.2.5.1 b) glosses the list as containing one entry for each route that could result in arrival at D; §3.2.6.9.1 licenses cessation of further computation as an implementation matter. Conformance below key 1 flips with the reading: ION’s entire 512/1054/1 profile is that flip; under cessation it is zero. Under completeness, visited-list pruning itself carries the 210/1 residual above. Resolving the degree of freedom means pinning list contents, not merely picking a clause; the completeness reading is the only resolution that pins them canonically.

**OWLT margin value.** The arithmetic is normative; the value is operator-chosen. A margin-zero deployment and ION’s configured-speed margin are equally conformant under the text. The 12.3% footprint on dsn_real_v1 is the measured cost of that deferral.

**Route class, pinned three ways.** §1.4 defines a route with contact reuse and node revisits permitted. §3.2.1 declares the contact graph acyclic. §3.2.6.10 generates routes as shortest paths through that graph — loopless by path definition. The generation class is strictly narrower than the defined class; implementations follow generation. At the selection optimum the narrowing is inert by loop erasure; in route-list generation semantics it is not.

**Acyclicity declaration.** §3.2.1 defines the contact graph as a conceptual directed acyclic graph whose edge rule is purely topological. Any bidirectional pair yields a 2-cycle. The declaration is false on every plan in all three corpora and on any network with a two-way link. Generation leans on path-ness to terminate in a graph the text wrongly promises is acyclic.

**Full-tuple tie storage.** Selection is action-deterministic at every tie the four-key order cannot break: a full four-key tie forces the same entry node, and §3.2.8.1.4 b) consumes only the entry. §3.2.8.1.2 charges volume against a stored route object that §3.2.8.1.4 a) 4) resolves only as “arbitrarily” at those ties. That channel is real (a constructed witness yields deliverability disagreement) and regime-indexed: inert under endpoint-sourced traffic on the measured corpora; under relay-sourced traffic it fires on 36 of 1000 corpus_v3 plans at contention 2.0 and 82 at 8.0 (depth-4-adjudicated entry splits), while dsn_real_v1 under the same relay protocol opens residue on 37 of 55 plans and fires on none. (Measured under the harness's operationalized EVL semantics — scheduled-start effective durations and first-byte inter-hop threading; a 2026-07-10 volume-axis differential found both to deviate from the letter of §3.2.6.3/§3.2.6.8.5, reconciliation in progress. The channel-existence witness compares like against like and is unaffected; the fire counts are semantics-scoped.)

The localization result of the source is that the differential between faithful implementations is an instrument that measures these textual degrees of freedom. The reliability risk, within the audited frame (same plan and dispatch stream, custody-free, single priority, no fragmentation), was never in the implementations; it is exactly where the text stops speaking.

## What would close it

The source names one-line fixes for each open point:

1. Pin candidate-list contents via the completeness reading of §3.2.5.1 b) (the only reading that pins them canonically), rather than leaving cessation as an unscoped implementation matter.
2. Publish a margin value or a declared grading frame for the OWLT margin of §3.2.6.5–6.
3. State the loopless restriction where §3.2.6.10 relies on path-ness, so the generation class matches what implementations compute.
4. Add the temporal guard the prose assumes to the contact-graph edge rule, or delete “acyclic” from §3.2.1 and state the loopless restriction where generation depends on it.
5. Pin the stored representative at full-tuple ties under §3.2.8.1.2 / §3.2.8.1.4 a) 4) (any canonical choice serves).

Fix the four deferred points and pin the stored tie representative, and two faithful implementations of this standard are behaviorally identical on every corpus, traffic shape, and contention level measured in the source, in selection and in delivery, from cislunar to interplanetary range.

## Claim basis

**Theorem (Lean 4, kernel-checked, zero `sorry`).** T1: returned routes are valid. T2a: the selected candidate is minimal in the full four-key order over the explored frontier. T2b: on plans with nonnegative OWLT, the returned arrival is globally optimal over the unbounded route class, contact-reusing competitors included, by loop erasure. Under the same nonnegative-OWLT hypothesis, the search returns `none` exactly when no valid route exists. T1 and T2a carry no plan hypothesis; T2b and completeness bind the nonnegative-OWLT hypothesis, discharged by construction on every ingested ionrc plan. Formal results establish key-1 globality over the unbounded valid-route class under nonnegative OWLT (T2b); keys 2–4 are correct over the explored frontier only.

**Empirical.** corpus_v3: 4093 found dispatches; four-construction table above; current reference 210 closing-caused key-3 and 1 key-4 residual; ION 4.1.4 profile 2526/512/1054/1. HDTN v2.0.0 (2026-07-10 differential, 1000 plans / 4100 queries): verdict 4100/4100 and arrival 4093/4093 agreement against both references; shorter equal-arrival witness on 2992/4093 (classic dijkstra, delta mean +2.51) and 527/4093 (cmr post-#69, delta mean +1.02); window-end probe set: one verdict-class and one arrival-class split, both code-pinned, reproduced on stock and post-#69 builds (filed as nasa/HDTN#70, #71). dsn_real_v1: 8912 dispatches (6935 found, 1977 none); reference conformant 6935/6935; ION 6905 conformant, key-3 30, key-4 0 in its declared margin frame (99.6%); margin moves delivered arrival on 851/6935 (12.3%). helio_regime_v1: reference conformant on 5016. Volume layer: endpoint-sourced traffic — channel inert on measured corpora; relay-sourced — 36/1000 and 82/1000 corpus_v3 plans at contention 2.0 and 8.0 (depth-4-adjudicated); dsn_real_v1 37/55 plans open residue, zero fire (volume numbers semantics-scoped; see the tie-storage paragraph). Artifacts: Lean reference and theorems; `scripts/diffharness/`; per-corpus reports under `out_diff_v3/` and `out_s5/`; volume-replay under `out_evl_relay/`; working notes under `docs/notes/`.

**Not claimed.** No statement about custody (including the §2.4.4 probe option), multi-priority traffic, fragmentation, adversarial traffic, arithmetic tolerance, or implementations not measured. No statement that any implementation is unreliable in operation. The audited frame is custody-free, single-priority, no fragmentation, same plan and dispatch stream to both sides. “Reliability” in the source means determinism and deliverability of selection under the standard’s own semantics in that frame, not end-to-end network resilience.
